01 / KNOWLEDGE AUDIT

See the system before building it.

We inventory sources, examine repeated work, identify risks, establish a baseline, and select the first measurable workflow.

Outcome

A clear view of where value is lost and what should change first.

Deliverable

Knowledge map, risk assessment, workflow recommendation, and implementation blueprint.

Result

A bounded decision—proceed, revise, or stop before unnecessary complexity.

02 / FOUNDATION BUILD

Create one trusted source.

Important knowledge gains relationships, ownership, approval status, privacy boundaries, and reuse rules.

Owned

The repository and operating knowledge belong to the client.

Governed

Authority and change rules are explicit.

Adapted

The environment fits the client's existing tools and constraints.

03 / WORKFLOW INSTALLATION

Make one valuable task repeatable.

A focused workflow retrieves the smallest relevant body of approved knowledge, assembles a bounded result, and exposes the sources and approval checkpoint.

04 / KNOWLEDGE OPERATIONS

Keep the system alive.

New work is captured, knowledge is reviewed, workflows improve, and drift is detected before trust erodes.

05 / TECHNOLOGY

Fits your environment.

The implementation may use governed files, Markdown, Obsidian, OKF, or another appropriate environment. Technology follows the knowledge model, privacy needs, and workflow—not the other way around.

Human authority is structural. AI proposes and assembles. The client approves consequential decisions.
