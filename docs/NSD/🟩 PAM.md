## Positioning Statement

Novel Consulting is best positioned as a **knowledge-systems implementation consultancy**, not an AI content agency or another knowledge-management software vendor.

Its primary promise is:

> Novel Consulting turns scattered intellectual property into governed, AI-assisted systems that retrieve approved knowledge and assemble trustworthy client deliverables.

The strongest initial wedge is **NKOS for consultants, trainers, coaches, and small professional firms** that repeatedly produce proposals, workshops, presentations, briefings, and reports. This audience has a recurring, observable workflow problem and a clearer ability to pay than the independent-author segment.

The two-product architecture is strategically coherent because NKOS and WRO Drafting share the same implementation engine—inventory, classification, knowledge modeling, retrieval, workflows, validation, and human approval—but they should retain separate sales narratives.

## Recommended Pilot

### Pilot customer

A 2–20 person consulting, coaching, or training firm that:

- Has at least three years of existing materials.
- Produces proposals or workshops at least monthly.
- Depends heavily on one founder’s memory.
- Already experiments with generative AI.
- Can disclose baseline workflow data.
- Will approve a case study if success criteria are met.

This closely matches the uploaded Ideal User profile and journey.

### Single workflow

**Proposal first-draft assembly**

Do not begin with all five proposed NKOS workflows. The initial implementation should retrieve approved service descriptions, methodologies, case studies, claims, and prior proposal components to produce a source-linked proposal draft.

### Single-metric test

**Primary metric:** Reduce preparation time for a credible first proposal draft by at least **50%**, without increasing unsupported or inaccurate claims.

### Supporting measurements

|Metric|Baseline|Pilot target|
|---|--:|--:|
|Time to first usable draft|Measure before build|≥50% reduction|
|Time spent searching for source material|Measure before build|≥60% reduction|
|Unsupported factual claims|Manual baseline|Zero|
|Required major revisions|Measure before build|≥30% reduction|
|Source traceability|Usually absent|100% of material claims linked|
|User confidence|1–5 score|≥4|
|Reuse after 30 days|None|At least 3 independent uses|

### Prototype-first artifact

The prototype should be a rough but usable workflow showing:

1. User request.
2. Retrieved approved sources.
3. Draft assembly.
4. Claim/source references.
5. Human approval checkpoint.
6. Revision and audit log.

No polished dashboard or proprietary platform is required.

## Role-Pass Workflow

The PAM and pilot should pass through the Synthetic Team workflow rather than being owned by a single undifferentiated AI role. The uploaded framework requires explicit roles, JOA agreements, and an Analyze → Draft → Challenge → Tighten → Align sequence.

|Pass|Role Card|Ownership|Known weakness|Signature move|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|Analyze|**Market Analyst / Truth-Teller**|Market evidence, benchmarks, assumptions|Can overvalue available quantitative data|Separates fact, assumption, and inference|
|Draft|**Knowledge Architect / Builder**|Repository model and prototype workflow|May overengineer structure|Builds the smallest reusable system|
|Challenge|**QA Reviewer**|Unsupported claims, privacy, failure modes|Can slow momentum|Runs JOA and kill-criteria checks|
|Tighten|**CMO / Narrative Strategist**|Positioning, buyer language, proof hierarchy|Can over-polish|Converts technical capability into an outcome signal|
|Align|**Client Facilitator / Operator**|Acceptance criteria, training, ownership, next step|May prioritize consensus over sharp choices|Confirms observable success with the client|

## Notes Linking Back to NSD

### NSD anchor

The core NSD promise is to transform scattered intellectual property into structured, governed, AI-assisted knowledge systems while leaving consequential decisions with the client.

The service architecture—Audit, Foundation Build, AI Workflow Implementation, Enablement and Governance, and Knowledge Operations—is internally consistent and suitable for a consulting-first launch.

The pricing approach correctly separates diagnostic, implementation, and ongoing operations and avoids hourly or per-document billing. However, price points still require validation.

### JOA alignment

**Justifiable**

- Begin with a recurring, verified workflow problem.
    
- Require baseline data before implementation.
    
- Build only the minimum knowledge structure needed for the selected workflow.
    

**Observable**

- Measure preparation time, search time, revision count, traceability, and user confidence.
    
- Display retrieved sources and approval checkpoints.
    
- Record workflow use after delivery.
    

**Auditable**

- Link material claims to approved source documents.
    
- Maintain version status, ownership, and approval history.
    
- Record model-generated additions separately from retrieved facts.
    

The uploaded agreement explicitly defines success in these terms: rapid knowledge location, approved retrieval, faster deliverable assembly, staff trust, and client maintainability.

## Kill Criteria

The existing NSD defines strong falsifiable conditions, including failure to secure three paid diagnostics within six months, failure to reduce preparation time, failure of clients to maintain their systems, and continued perception as an AI content agency.

For operational clarity, apply these binary tests:

|Test|Pass condition|Fail condition|
|---|---|---|
|Paid demand|3 paid diagnostics within 6 months|Fewer than 3|
|Pilot outcome|≥50% reduction in the primary workflow’s preparation time|Less than 25% reduction across both pilots|
|Quality|Equal or better human-rated output with zero unsupported material claims|Material accuracy declines|
|Repeat use|Client uses workflow at least 3 times within 30 days|Workflow is abandoned|
|Conversion|At least 1 of first 3 diagnostics converts to implementation|Zero conversions|
|Maintenance|Client assigns an owner and completes one update cycle|No owner or update activity|
|Positioning|Most qualified prospects describe the offer as a system or implementation service|Most describe it as content generation|
|Delivery economics|Gross delivery margin can exceed 50% after the third engagement|Custom work continues to consume nearly all revenue|

## Final Recommendation

**Proceed—with a narrow pilot and evidence gate.**

Novel Consulting has the ingredients of a credible specialist consultancy:

- A real structural customer problem.
    
- A large reachable professional-services market.
    
- A coherent consulting methodology.
    
- A clear anti-position against generic AI content generation.
    
- A governance story suited to increasing concern about unreliable AI output.
    
- A potential recurring-services layer.
    

It does not yet have sufficient evidence for a broad launch, software build, fundraising thesis, or simultaneous two-market campaign.

The next investment should be in **one paid NKOS diagnostic and one proposal-workflow prototype**, followed by a source-linked case study. The decisive question is not whether the category sounds compelling. It is whether the system produces a materially faster, more trustworthy deliverable and whether a customer will pay to keep using it.
