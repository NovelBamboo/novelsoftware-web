# 🌟 KISS — Keep Idea Summary Simple

## Novel Consulting / Novel Knowledge Operating System

### 1. WHO is it for? 🎯

It is for authors and expert-led businesses whose valuable knowledge is scattered across drafts, documents, folders, presentations, notes, emails, and personal memory.

The primary users are:

- Authors, publishers, and writing teams developing long-form fiction.
    
- Consultants, trainers, coaches, and small professional firms that repeatedly create proposals, workshops, presentations, reports, and client deliverables.
    

They benefit most when their work depends on specialist knowledge that is difficult to find, reuse, transfer, or explain to generic AI tools.

---

### 2. WHAT does it do? 🔧

Novel Consulting organizes a client’s intellectual property into a structured knowledge system that AI can use reliably.

WRO Drafting helps authors develop, draft, review, and revise stories while protecting continuity, canon, voice, and creative intent.

NKOS helps expert-led businesses reuse approved knowledge to create proposals, workshops, presentations, briefings, and other client deliverables faster and more consistently.

In simple terms:

It helps people stop recreating work they have already done.

---

### 3. WHEN is it used? 🕒

It is used when a person or team needs to turn existing knowledge into a new deliverable.

Authors use it while planning a story, outlining chapters, preparing scenes, checking continuity, drafting, and revising.

Businesses use it while responding to an opportunity, preparing for a client meeting, drafting a proposal, designing a workshop, creating a presentation, or onboarding someone into the firm’s methods.

It is used regularly, especially whenever the user would otherwise search through old files, rebuild material from memory, or repeatedly explain context to an AI assistant.

---

### 4. WHERE do they use it? 📍

Clients use it in their normal working environment: on desktop or laptop computers, alongside documents, cloud drives, notes, slides, manuscripts, templates, and project materials.

The structured repository may live in a client-appropriate environment such as Markdown, Obsidian, OKF, or another governed knowledge platform.

The system connects the client’s source material to focused AI-assisted workflows. It should fit into existing work rather than forcing the client to abandon familiar tools and processes.

---

### 5. WHY does it matter? ❤️

Valuable expertise loses value when it cannot be found, trusted, reused, or transferred.

Without a structured system:

- Authors lose continuity and creative control.
    
- Businesses repeatedly rebuild proposals and presentations.
    
- Important knowledge remains trapped in one person’s memory.
    
- AI produces generic or invented material.
    
- Client delivery becomes slower and less consistent.
    
- Teams cannot easily trace where claims or decisions came from.
    

Novel Consulting matters because it protects human knowledge while making that knowledge more useful.

The practical benefit is faster, more consistent work.

The emotional benefit is confidence: clients know their stories, methods, evidence, and decisions remain under their control.

---

### 6. HOW does it work? 🧭

First, Novel Consulting inventories the client’s existing material and identifies the knowledge, workflows, duplication, gaps, risks, and highest-value use cases.

Second, the material is classified and organized into a structured repository. Important items are given clear relationships, ownership, authority, status, privacy rules, and reuse conditions.

Third, focused AI workflows are created for valuable tasks such as drafting a chapter outline, checking continuity, assembling a proposal, building a workshop, retrieving evidence, or creating a presentation outline.

The AI retrieves the smallest relevant set of approved knowledge, then proposes or assembles a draft.

The human reviews and approves the result.

A suitable welcome-mat message is:

> What are you trying to produce today? I’ll use your approved knowledge, show the sources I relied on, and leave the final decision with you.
