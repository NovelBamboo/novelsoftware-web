# 👤 Ideal User Profile & Journey

# 🎯 Primary User — Consultant / Expert-Led Business

## Demographics

- Consultant, trainer, coach, advisor, or boutique professional firm.
    
- Owns years of presentations, workshops, proposals, reports, and client material.
    
- Small team where expertise depends heavily on one or two people.
    
- Regularly creates custom client deliverables.
    

## Motivations

- Stop recreating existing work.
    
- Deliver proposals and workshops faster.
    
- Scale expertise beyond one individual.
    
- Improve consistency across engagements.
    
- Make AI useful using real business knowledge rather than generic prompts.
    

## Behaviors

- Searches through folders before every engagement.
    
- Copies material from previous proposals.
    
- Rewrites presentations that already exist.
    
- Keeps valuable knowledge in personal memory.
    
- Uses AI but spends significant time correcting generic output.
    

## Needs (Win Conditions)

The project succeeds when the business can:

- Retrieve approved expertise quickly.
    
- Assemble first drafts from trusted material.
    
- Reduce proposal and workshop preparation time.
    
- Preserve organizational knowledge as the business grows.
    
- Deliver consistently regardless of who prepares the work.
    

---

# 🛤 Primary User Journey — Expert-Led Business

## Stage 1 — Knowledge Audit

**Goal**

Understand where expertise currently exists.

**Action**

Participate in interviews, workflow reviews, and source inventory.

**System Response**

Novel Consulting produces a Knowledge Systems Audit highlighting duplication, risks, opportunities, and implementation priorities.

---

## Stage 2 — Foundation Build

**Goal**

Organize business knowledge into a reusable operating system.

**Action**

Services, methodologies, case studies, templates, workshops, and client material are classified and structured.

**System Response**

A governed knowledge repository becomes the firm's trusted source of expertise.

---

## Stage 3 — AI Workflow Implementation

**Goal**

Automate valuable client-delivery activities.

**Action**

Staff request proposal drafts, workshop outlines, presentation structures, client briefings, or case-study retrieval.

**System Response**

AI assembles deliverables from approved business knowledge while maintaining traceability and consistency.

---

## Stage 4 — Knowledge Operations

**Goal**

Keep the system valuable over time.

**Action**

The business adds new projects, improves workflows, and trains new team members.

**System Response**

The repository remains current, reusable, and aligned with evolving services while reducing knowledge loss.

---

# 🎤 Secondary User — Independent Author

## Demographics

- Individual author or small writing team
    
- Working on one or more long-form fiction projects
    
- Comfortable using digital writing tools but overwhelmed by scattered notes
    
- Owns significant creative intellectual property
    

## Motivations

- Finish a manuscript without losing momentum.
    
- Maintain story continuity across hundreds of pages.
    
- Protect creative voice while using AI responsibly.
    
- Reduce revision cycles without sacrificing quality.
    
- Build a repeatable writing process for future books.
    

## Behaviors

- Keeps story information across multiple documents, notebooks, and folders.
    
- Frequently searches for character details or past events.
    
- Uses AI for brainstorming but spends time correcting inconsistencies.
    
- Revisits earlier chapters to verify continuity.
    
- Creates duplicate notes because existing material is difficult to locate.
    

## Needs (Win Conditions)

The project is successful when the author can:

- Find any story information within minutes.
    
- Trust story continuity across the manuscript.
    
- Produce new chapters without repeatedly re-explaining the story.
    
- Keep AI aligned with the author's established canon and voice.
    
- Spend more time writing than organizing.
    

---

# 🛤 Secondary User Journey — Author

## Stage 1 — Discovery

**Goal**

Improve the writing process without giving up creative control.

**Action**

Books a Knowledge Systems Audit and discusses their current writing workflow.

**System Response**

Novel Consulting identifies scattered story assets, continuity risks, duplicated work, and opportunities for AI-assisted workflows.

---

## Stage 2 — Foundation Build

**Goal**

Create one trusted source for the story.

**Action**

Story assets, canon, characters, worldbuilding, outlines, and manuscripts are organized into a structured knowledge system.

**System Response**

The author receives a navigable story repository with clear relationships between every major creative element.

---

## Stage 3 — Writing & Revision

**Goal**

Write new material with confidence.

**Action**

The author requests chapter outlines, scene preparation, continuity reviews, or bounded revisions.

**System Response**

AI retrieves approved story knowledge, proposes drafts or reviews, and references established canon while leaving creative decisions to the author.

---

## Stage 4 — Growth

**Goal**

Maintain the system throughout the life of the project.

**Action**

New chapters, revisions, and story assets are added as the manuscript evolves.

**System Response**

The knowledge base grows alongside the manuscript, preserving continuity and reducing future effort.

---

# ✅ Value Summary
## For Expert-Led Businesses

**Capture → Structure → Retrieve → Deliver → Improve**

Businesses transform accumulated expertise into a reusable competitive advantage instead of repeatedly rebuilding it.

---
## For Authors

**Create → Organize → Write → Revise → Publish**

Authors spend less time searching and more time creating.
